What we look for in a property

Not every property is a fit. That's by design.

Our families come for community as much as for the place. The properties that work best aren't always the prettiest — they're the ones with room for kids to run, food that nourishes, and a host team that gets it.

✓Capacity for 8–12 families

Roughly 30–50 people. A mix of family suites, double rooms, and ideally a couple of single rooms for facilitators or solo educators.

✓Outdoor space that breathes

Garden, farm, beach, forest. Kids need to run. Families need to wander. A four-walled hotel doesn't work — even a beautiful one.

✓A real shared kitchen or food story

Either a kitchen we can use together, or a food program that's actually special — local, seasonal, with a story behind it.

✓A communal heart

Somewhere to eat together. Somewhere to gather at night. Somewhere a kids' learning space can live for the month.

✓A host team with values aligned

You don't have to be running a coliving — but you need to be the kind of place that delights in families being there, not tolerates them.

✓Shoulder-season availability

We're not competing with your high season. We fill the off and shoulder weeks — when occupancy is hardest and our families travel cheapest.

The partnership

What we hold. What you hold.

We operate in clear roles. You shouldn't have to become a community organizer overnight.

What we hold

  • Family vetting + curation (the cohort fits together before they arrive)
  • Booking, payments, deposits, refunds — handled centrally
  • Dedicated on-site Facilitator who holds the social and emotional container
  • Educator / Master Teacher who runs the kids' learning space
  • Pre-arrival packs, group communication, parent circles, ritual design
  • Weekly check-ins with you to flag anything before it grows

What you hold

  • The place — your land, your buildings, your team's care
  • Accommodation, cleaning, kitchen ops, the daily rhythm of the property
  • One named contact who liaises with our Facilitator
  • The story of where you are — local food, local people, local nature
  • A clear pricing structure for shoulder-season group bookings
