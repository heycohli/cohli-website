Why Bali, why now

Bali is the most-requested destination in our 1,500-family community. It has been consistently, across 2025 and into 2026 planning. The appeal is specific: warm weather out of the European / North American winter, strong worldschool and nomadic-family culture already in place, a food and wellness ecosystem families already know and trust, and properties that understand slow living.

We're not looking to do Bali in peak season (August, New Year). We're looking to build cohorts in the shoulder windows — Feb–Apr and Sep–Nov — where our families travel and your properties most need coherent group bookings.

"Our families don't want a villa. They want to wake up somewhere that feels like it's teaching them something. Bali keeps coming up because it already does that."

What we're looking for in a Bali property

Bali has enormous range. We're being specific about what fits a Cohli cohort.

  • Nature-led setting. Rice fields, jungle edge, cliff and ocean, or a genuine eco-village — not a compound surrounded by traffic.
  • Capacity for 8–12 family units. Usually that's 15–25 rooms across family suites, doubles, and a few singles.
  • A shared heart — an open-air communal space where 40 people can eat and gather. Bali-style open pavilion works beautifully.
  • Kitchen capacity to feed the group, either through your own team, a partnered warung, or a local chef network we can co-design with.
  • Space for a kids' learning area — a dedicated open room or shaded platform where a Master Teacher can hold a group of 10–15 children daily.
  • A pool, water feature, or safe swimming access. Non-negotiable for families with small kids.
  • Host team that is excited about families, not merely tolerant of them. This is the single biggest fit signal.

Regions we're open to (in priority order)

We'll consider well-run properties across the island. Based on family asks, this is our current prioritization:

  • Ubud + surrounds — rice terraces, creative community, wellness depth. Highest demand.
  • North + Central highlands (Munduk, Sidemen, Kintamani) — cooler, quieter, more nature-immersive.
  • East coast (Amed, Candidasa) — diving, calm ocean, real villages.
  • West coast (Pemuteran, Menjangan edge) — slower, reef-adjacent, underexplored by families.
  • Canggu / Berawa — surf-oriented cohorts. We're more selective here — heavy traffic and noise are friction for families with young kids.

What a partnership with Cohli involves

We're transparent about what we bring and what we need, so this is a real conversation not a pitch.

Shoulder-season commitment

A block of availability (4–12 weeks) in one of our target windows, ideally across 2026 and optionally into 2027 if the first cohort lands well.

A single point of contact

Someone on your team who liaises with our on-site Facilitator and our Cohli Ops lead. Not a whole new job — a named human.

Transparent group pricing

A clear shoulder-season rate for the full cohort — accommodation, any meals included, extras. We pay as a single entity; you don't manage 30 bookings.

Flexibility on rhythm

Our families keep the property's rhythm. But we need to design our own rituals — opening circles, weekly parent circles, kids' learning sessions. A collaborative frame, not rigid hotel protocols.

In exchange, you get a full-property shoulder-season booking with families who treat the land with care, a dedicated facilitator who solves people-issues before they reach you, and — if the partnership clicks — a recurring relationship rather than a one-off transaction.
