Why we're excited about Alentejo

Portugal's Alentejo region has been calling to us for years. It's slower than the coast, warmer than the north, and the land feels alive in a way that's hard to describe. Rolling cork forests, wild olive trees, and families who've lived off this soil for generations.

This eco-farm is different from Casa Sumapaz. It's not a built retreat center—it's a working farmstead with its own rhythm. The houses have more independence, the kids program is lighter and nature-based, and the vibe is less structured and more... organic.

We wanted a place where families could breathe a little easier. Where you could cook your own meals on weekends if you wanted to. Where slow living isn't a concept—it's just how the place works. Where the kids could roam and the adults could work from a quiet terrace overlooking vineyards.

This is early days, and we're still learning the rhythms of the property. But we're committed to showing up and discovering it together with the first families who join us.

The Place
A sustainable farmhouse in Portugal's heart

The Alentejo Eco-Farm is a working agricultural property in southern Portugal, where cork forests meet vineyards and the pace of life slows down. The property features a cluster of thoughtfully designed accommodations—Farmlofts and WeWood houses with private kitchens, modern amenities, and direct access to gardens, pools, and trails. Each home is independent yet connected to shared spaces where the community gathers.

Accommodation

Private houses in the Núcleo Cravos cluster: Farmlofts and WeWood houses (65–85m²) with fully equipped kitchens, AC, integrated heating, WiFi, coffee machine, and private terrace. Weekly cleaning with fresh linens included. Premium Quinteiro WeWood houses (85m²) also available.

The Surroundings

Located in the heart of Alentejo's wine and cork country. Minutes from hiking trails through native forest, biking routes across farmland, and local villages. The region is known for its slow rhythm, natural hot springs, and farm-to-table food culture. One of Europe's best-kept secrets for families seeking authentic slowness.

What's Included
Your stay at the Eco-Farm

We've designed this to balance structure and freedom. Breakfast is shared; weekends are yours.

🏠

Your Own House

Fully equipped private home with AC, heating, WiFi, kitchen, and terrace. Weekly cleaning with fresh linens. All the independence you need, plus community when you want it.

🍲

Farmtable Breakfast Daily

Fresh, seasonal breakfast served each morning at the main table. Weekday lunches and dinners available à la carte (€15–30/person). Weekends are self-catered—your house has everything you need to cook.

🎓

Nature-Based Kids Program

Weekday mornings (9am–5pm) at the on-site Nature Interpretation Centre. Two Cohli educators lead nature activities, exploration, and learning using the property's forests, gardens, and streams. 1:7 ratio.

💻

Pools, Sauna & Wellness

Access to exterior and interior pools, wood-fired sauna, jacuzzi, cold plunge pool, and bonfire area. Plus a well-equipped gym and quiet coworking spaces with strong WiFi for focused work.

🌍

Activities & Exploration

Guided hiking and biking on the property and surrounding trails. Horseback riding (Volteio a Cavalo), garden visits (Visita à Horta), painting circles (Hora de Pintar), and cooking classes (Mini Chefes).

👥

Community Dinners

Weekly farmtable community pizza dinners bring families together. Optional weeknight shared meals are also available. Designed to deepen connection without obligating every meal.

Weekday lunch + dinner: €30/adult, €15/child (4–11), €10/child (1–3). Weekly community pizza: €16/adult, €12/child (4–12). Kids program lunch: €15/child (4–11), €10 (1–3).
