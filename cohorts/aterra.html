Coming home to Portugal, together

Portugal is home for Tiago. Not just technically — it's the place that shaped how he thinks about family, food, and community. When we found Aterra, nestled in the woodlands of the Alentejo coast, we knew this was where we wanted to bring Cohli families next.

Aterra isn't a hotel. It's a living project built around permaculture ethics: earth care, people care, fair shares. The yurts, tipis, and cabins sit between fruit trees and grasslands, and the freshwater lake is the kind of place where kids lose track of time completely.

What we love about this place is the pace. Portuguese summer has a rhythm — long warm days, slow meals, the sound of cicadas. It's the kind of environment where families relax into each other. Where conversations happen naturally over dinner and kids build friendships while catching frogs by the lake.

That said, living in a glamping retreat with other families isn't always comfortable. Some mornings are hot. Some kids clash over the surfboards. Some parents need more quiet than others. We've learned to design the structure that holds all of it — beauty and mess alike.

If you've been dreaming of a summer where your family connects with something deeper — with nature, with other families, with Portugal itself — this is the one. Come as you are.

The Place
An ecological retreat where nature leads

Aterra is an ecological retreat and glamping space in the Alentejo coastal region of Portugal. Built around permaculture ethics of earth care, people care, and fair shares, the space has been thoughtfully designed. Imagine your family waking up surrounded by peaceful woodlands, fruit trees, and grasslands — with the Portuguese coast a short drive away.

Six Accommodation Types

Yurts, bell tents, cabins, tipis, a wooden chalet, and Silvia the Airstream. Each sleeps 4-6 people with private bathrooms or nearby shared facilities. All feature eco-conscious touches, fresh linens, and your own retreat space. Prices range from €4,600 to €6,500 for the full 27-day stay.

The Surroundings

Southern Portugal's Alentejo coast is known for unspoiled beaches, cork oak forests, and slow village life. The private freshwater lake is where kids lose track of time. Within 20 minutes: Atlantic beaches perfect for surfing and swimming. The region is warm and dry in July, creating ideal conditions for outdoor living and family connection.

Daily Rhythm
What a Wednesday looks like

There's a rhythm, not a rigid schedule. Some days flow differently. This is the shape of a typical midweek day.

7:30 AM

Slow Morning

Breakfast is laid out — fresh fruit, bread, coffee, Portuguese pastries. Some families eat early by the lake, others drift in. The cicadas are already singing.

9:00 AM

Learning Circle

Autumn gathers the kids by age group. Today: nature journaling around the permaculture gardens, identifying plants and insects. Younger ones do sensory play in the grass.

11:00 AM

Lake Time & Exploration

The freshwater lake is the heart of Aterra. Kids swim, build rafts, catch frogs. Parents bring a book or join in. The property is big enough for everyone to find their own corner.

1:00 PM

Shared Lunch

For families who opted into the meal plan, the chef serves locally sourced Portuguese food under the trees. Others use the communal kitchen to make their own. Saturday is pizza night.

2:00 PM

Rest / Free Time

Quiet hour. Younger kids nap in the yurts. Older ones play board games in the shade. Some parents disappear for a solo walk through the cork oak forest.

3:00 PM

Parent Work Block

Starlink kicks in. Parents work from the communal areas or their own spaces. Kids have supervised play, art stations, and outdoor games.

5:30 PM

Community Activity

Today: a visit to the local village market. Tomorrow: Portuguese language basics for the kids. Friday: a parent skill-share under the stars.

7:30 PM

Dinner & Evening

Long Portuguese dinner as the sun sets. Kids play until bedtime. Parents gather with wine and conversation that goes deeper than small talk. The stars here are extraordinary.

Getting There
How to reach Aterra
Interactive map: Lisbon / Faro → Aterra (Alentejo coast)

Nearest Airports

Lisbon (LIS) or Faro (FAO). Both have direct flights from across Europe and beyond. Faro is closer to the Alentejo coast.

Transfer

About 1.5-2 hours drive from Lisbon or Faro. We can help coordinate shared transport or you can rent a car (recommended for exploring the region).

Visa

Portugal is part of the Schengen Area. EU citizens need no visa. US, UK, Canadian, and many other nationalities get 90-day visa-free access. We'll share specific guidance after acceptance.

Before You Go
What to know before arriving

Practical tips for making the most of your Portuguese summer.

🌡
Sun protection. July in the Alentejo is warm and sunny (25-30°C). Bring hats, sunscreen, and light layers for cooler evenings.
👞
Swimwear for days. Between the freshwater lake and nearby beaches, your kids will live in swimsuits. Pack plenty.
🔌
European adapter. Portugal uses Type F outlets (round pins). US/UK families will need adapters.
📷
A camera you'll actually use. The golden hour light in the Alentejo is something else. Phone is fine — just capture it.
📚
A few books to share. Long evenings by the fire pit call for good reading. Leave one, take one from the communal shelf.
💊
Insect repellent. Woodland living in summer means mosquitoes at dusk. Natural repellent works well here.
