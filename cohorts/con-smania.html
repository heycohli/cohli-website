Finding home in the Blue Zone

Costa Rica called to us for reasons we couldn't quite articulate at first. But when we stepped into Con Smania — a family-friendly eco-retreat nestled on the Nicoya Peninsula, one of the world's Blue Zones — something shifted. This isn't a beach resort. It's a community built around intentional living, wellness, and the kind of slowness that modern families rarely experience.

What strikes you first about Con Smania is the light. Golden, diffuse, filtered through centuries-old trees. The air smells like ocean and earth. Everything moves a little slower here — not because of laziness, but because the rhythm of the place invites you into presence. Kids lose track of time exploring tide pools. Parents find themselves in deeper conversations than they've had in years.

The Nicoya Peninsula is famous for longevity — communities where people live past 100, still gardening, still laughing, still connected. We wanted our families to taste that. To understand what it actually feels like to prioritize wellbeing, real food, and genuine human connection. To raise our kids in an environment where health and relationship aren't side projects — they're the foundation.

Living with other families in close quarters is real. Some mornings are hot. Some kids clash over attention. Some parents crave more solitude. We don't pretend that away. Instead, we build the structure that holds the whole spectrum — the magic and the messiness, the rest and the adventure.

If you're searching for what family could feel like beyond the daily grind — where your kids see you more present, where friendships deepen, where you remember why you wanted to raise a family in the first place — this cohort captures that possibility.

The Place
A Blue Zone retreat where wellness lives

Con Smania is a family-friendly eco-retreat on the Nicoya Peninsula, one of the world's five Blue Zones — regions where people live the longest, healthiest lives. Set in lush tropical gardens just a five-minute walk from the beach, Con Smania embodies principles of wellness, sustainability, and heart-centered community. This is where wellness stops being aspirational and becomes simply how you live.

The Niches Retreat

Con Smania is nestled within centuries-old trees on land that has held families and community for generations. Private casitas blend modern comfort with natural materials, each with air conditioning, Starlink WiFi, and a direct connection to the surrounding ecosystem. Your family has its own space to retreat to, while community spaces invite deeper connection.

Nicoya Peninsula Living

The Nicoya Peninsula is known for longevity, vitality, and a lifestyle that prioritizes wellbeing. You're minutes from world-class beaches, surrounded by nature, and embedded in a community that understands the rhythms of sustainable, joyful living. Pura vida isn't just a slogan here — it's the actual shape of daily life.

Daily Rhythm
What a Wednesday feels like

There's a rhythm, not a rigid schedule. Some days flow differently. This is the shape of a typical day — built around presence, nourishment, and gentle intention.

6:30 AM

Sunrise Awakening

The light arrives before the heat. Some families wake early for the beach. Others still sleep. The morning is yours — coffee on the patio, kids playing in the garden, the sound of birds and ocean in the distance.

8:00 AM

Breakfast Together

Fresh tropical fruits, eggs, local bread, juice from the smoothie bar. Communal table, but no pressure. Families drift in and out. Conversations happen naturally.

9:00 AM

Kids Programming

On-site education circles by age group. Today: herbal workshop where kids learn about local plants. Tomorrow: art and nature. The facilitator family joins in, modeling curiosity and play.

11:00 AM

Beach or Garden Time

Five-minute walk to the beach. Tide pools, swimming, sand play. Or stay in the garden — reading, resting, exploring. Kids lose track of time in the best way.

1:00 PM

Shared Lunch

Locally sourced, fresh ingredients prepared with care. Vegetables from local farmers, fresh fish, rice, salads. Eating becomes a gathering, not a transaction.

2:00 PM

Rest & Quiet Hour

The heat peaks. Younger kids nap in air-conditioned casitas. Older ones rest, read, or play quietly. Parents use this time for work, a solo walk, or their own restoration.

3:30 PM

Parent Work Block

WiFi is reliable. Workspace available. Kids have supervised activities and play. You can work knowing they're safe and engaged.

5:30 PM

Community Gathering

Weekly spaces for real conversation. Or just time to walk with other parents, share what's landing. A wellness workshop, a skill-share, a deeper circle.

7:30 PM

Evening & Starlight

Dinner with families who want to share. Kids play as the sun sets. The sky here has more stars than you remember. Long conversations under them. The kind of evening that makes you understand why people stay.

Getting There
Samara, on the Nicoya Peninsula
San Jose / Liberia → Samara, Guanacaste

Nearest Airports

San Jose (SJO) — international hub. Or Liberia (LIR) — closer regional option. Both connect to Samara via short flight or scenic drive.

Transfer

About 4-5 hours from San Jose, 1.5-2 hours from Liberia. Road is beautiful — transition from highlands to beach. Direct shuttle services available, or rent a car to explore.

Visa

US, UK, Canadian, and many other nationalities get 180-day visa-free access. We'll provide specific guidance and resources post-commitment.

Before You Go
What to know about living in Costa Rica

Practical wisdom for settling into the Nicoya Peninsula.

🌡
Sun is serious. UV here is intense (26-28°C year-round). Reef-safe sunscreen, hats, and light long sleeves for afternoon hours are non-negotiable for kids.
👞
Swimwear, plural. Beach access, pools, daily heat. Kids live in swimsuits. Bring more than you think you'll need.
🔌
US adapter (Type A). Costa Rica uses the same outlets as the US. Simple to power everything.
📷
Waterproof camera case. Tide pools, beach time, water play. Your phone works, but a case protects it. Golden hour light is extraordinary.
📚
Books to share. Long, slow evenings call for good reading. Leave one in the community library, take one home.
🍰
Insect repellent (natural). Coastal living has mosquitoes at dusk. DEET-free options work well on sensitive skin.
