Why we created ONZE

We started Cohli because we believed that slow family travel could be transformative. Casa Sumapaz proved it. But not every family wants mountains and meditation circles. Some families want luxury. They want space. They want a private chef and a cenote and the kind of quiet that costs something to protect.

ONZE is for those families. It's for the parents who know their kids thrive when they slow down, but who also know that "roughing it" isn't what makes them feel at home. It's for families who travel at a high level and expect things like good design, excellent food, and curated community—not because they're snobs, but because they know the difference.

This isn't a retreat. It's a sanctuary. A place where the only structure is the one that holds you. Where your children play with 12 other kids in a jungle they already feel they own by week one. Where the biggest challenge isn't logistics or discomfort—it's knowing when to put your work down and swim in an underground cenote in the afternoon.

We've spent months finding and designing this space. The hosts understand luxury hospitality. The location is protected and wild. The meal program is what you'd pay $500/night for at a resort, except it's yours for a month, shared with people you actually like.

This is Cohli for families who want belonging and elegance.

The Place
A private jungle sanctuary in the Riviera Maya

ONZE Xpu Há is a 10-acre private sanctuary set between Playa del Carmen and Tulum, designed for families who want to slow down without sacrificing comfort. Seven beautifully designed villas, a private crystal-clear cenote, swimming pool, and a dedicated yoga pavilion. Every detail—from the local artisan furnishings to the sustainable architecture—reflects a philosophy of elegance with purpose.

The Concept

"We believe movement is normal, but moving alone can be the challenge. For families who live with mobility, the hardest thing to find isn't a beautiful destination. It is the space to slow down. We crave the steady, quiet rhythm of a village." This is a micro-village for 30 days—not a highly-scheduled retreat. A shared rhythm that feels restful and luxurious.

The Reality

This residency takes place in an off-grid sanctuary during August. It is also storm season in the Riviera Maya, which means the weather may be unpredictable. We design around this—covered spaces, flexible scheduling, and a team ready for anything.

Practical Tips
What to pack & prepare

Lessons from families who've done jungle living before.

🌡
Light layers. Hot and humid during the day, cooler at night. Breathable fabrics for the heat.
👞
Waterproof gear. Lightweight rain jacket, waterproof bag for electronics. Storm season means afternoon showers.
🔌
Universal adapter. Mexico uses Type A/B outlets (same as US). But bring adapters if you're coming from elsewhere.
📷
Camera + underwater case. The cenote, the sunsets, the jungle moments. Worth capturing.
🌓
Good bug spray. Mosquitos exist. Bring what you trust. (The sanctuary provides some, but your preferred brand is nice to have.)
🍅
Medications & supplements. We have a first aid station, but if you take specific things, bring enough for 30 days + buffer.
💶
Reef-safe sunscreen. Out of respect for the cenote and the surrounding ecosystem.
📚
Something to share. A book, a game, a skill. The community library and skill-share nights are real.
