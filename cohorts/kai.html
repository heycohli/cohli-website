The Sacred Valley called us here

There's something about the Sacred Valley that stays with you. The light hits the mountains at a certain angle, and time feels different. When we discovered Residencia Kai, we felt it immediately — this is where art and family can live together.

Kai Art Residency isn't a hotel. It's a living studio in the heart of the Arin community, built with respect for local materials, local knowledge, and the sacred land itself. Four houses sit among gardens and workshops, with views that inspire creativity simply by existing.

We imagined your family here: kids exploring the landscape as their classroom, parents finding creative flow in art studios, everyone learning from the artisans and traditions that make this region magical. The Incan ruins are walking distance. The markets pulse with color. The mountains hold stories.

Finding the right timing to bring Cohli families to Peru has been the challenge — but we're committed to making this happen. We're working with Kai to find the perfect season, to ensure travel is smooth, and to create something genuinely special for families who want to go deeper.

If the Sacred Valley is calling you too, join us on the waitlist. We'll be in touch as soon as we lock in dates. This one is worth the wait.

The Place
Where art and mountains meet family

Residencia Kai is an arts and research residence in the heart of Peru's Sacred Valley. Built with local materials and designed with deep respect for the land and its people, Kai creates spaces that honor both creativity and community. Four houses sit among studios, gardens, and open land — with the Andes rising all around you.

The Houses

Four thoughtfully designed family houses: Wayra, Nina, Yacu, and Jallpa. Each with its own kitchen, private bedrooms, and warm character. Built with local materials and attention to detail. Ready for families to make home.

The Sacred Valley

High in the Andes at 3,400 meters, the Sacred Valley is a place where Incan heritage lives alongside contemporary artistic vision. Markets burst with color. Ruins crown the hillsides. Indigenous communities preserve ancient traditions. It's a landscape that opens the heart.

Daily Rhythm
What a day in the Sacred Valley looks like

There's a rhythm shaped by mountains, light, and creative energy. This is the shape of a typical day at Kai.

6:30 AM

Mountain Wake

Early light fills the valleys. The Andes are already alive with color. Coffee, tea, and bread on your porch. Some families wake early to watch the sunrise paint the mountains.

8:00 AM

Kids Program Begins

Kids gather for activities — nature walks, local art workshops, exploring the grounds, language practice. The landscape is their classroom. Parents have quiet time or studio access.

10:00 AM

Studio Time / Work Blocks

Creative parents head to studios. Others catch up on work calls (decent internet at Kai). The community house is open — some families work together, some seek solitude.

12:30 PM

Lunch

Optional chef services or families cook in their own kitchens. Some days the community gathers in the big kitchen. Fresh local ingredients, slow meals, conversations that linger.

2:00 PM

Rest & Exploration

Afternoon siesta for younger kids. Older ones might hike nearby trails, visit local artisans, or play in the expansive grounds. Some families venture to nearby towns or ruins.

4:00 PM

Creative Projects

Kids work on art projects, craft activities, or nature study. Parents continue studio work or family time. The light at this hour is extraordinary.

6:30 PM

Community Time

Kids play in the green space as day winds down. Parents gather for wine and conversation. Sometimes a shared dinner, sometimes families retire to their houses.

8:00 PM

Evening & Night

Kids to bed early (altitude and mountain air make for deep sleep). Adults may gather by firelight, read, journal, or simply sit with the stars. The night sky here is unforgettable.

Getting There
How to reach the Sacred Valley
Interactive map: Cusco / Lima → Arin, Sacred Valley, Peru

Nearest Airports

Cusco (CUZ) or Lima (LIM). Cusco is the gateway to the Sacred Valley and closer to Kai. Direct or 1-stop flights from major US and European cities.

Transfer

About 1.5-2 hours drive from Cusco to Kai. High altitude at 3,400m — we recommend arriving a day early to acclimatize, or getting to altitude gradually.

Visa & Altitude

Most nationalities get 90-day visa-free entry to Peru. Plan for mild altitude adjustment. We'll share complete travel guidance once dates are confirmed.

Before You Go
What to know about the Sacred Valley

Practical and important details for living in the mountains of Peru.

⛰️
Altitude adjustment. Kai is at 3,400m (11,000ft). Most people feel fine, but plan an extra day or two in Cusco to acclimatize. Stay hydrated, move slowly the first days.
☀️
Sun at altitude. The sun is intense in the mountains. Sunscreen, hats, and shade-seeking are essential. Temperatures vary: 15-22°C depending on season and time of day.
🧥
Layers, always. Mountain weather shifts throughout the day. Mornings are cool, afternoons warm, evenings crisp. Pack layers you can mix and match.
💧
Water and health. Drink plenty of water for altitude. We'll provide guidance on local water quality and health precautions. Travel insurance that covers altitude is wise.
📷
Capture the magic. The light in the Sacred Valley is extraordinary. Bring a camera you love (phone is perfect). The ruins, the markets, the faces of local artisans — there are stories everywhere.
📚
Openness to the place. The Sacred Valley is a living landscape with deep indigenous roots. Approach with respect, curiosity, and humility. Listen to the stories locals share. Honor the land.
